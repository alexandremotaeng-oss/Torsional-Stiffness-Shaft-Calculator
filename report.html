Conclusion

  • ⚠️ Critical segment: Segment #8 (Ø 24.5 mm × 85.6 mm) — this segment will be the first to reach plastic deformation under torsional load, due to its lowest torsional capacity in the assembly.
  • 🔩 Maximum torque without plastic deformation: 349.9 N·m. At this torque, the critical segment reaches the shear yield limit. The corresponding elastic twist angle of the critical segment is 0.612°.
  • ✅ Recommended safe operating range: 0 to 209.9 N·m (60% of the elastic limit, safety factor ≈ 1.67). Within this range, all segments remain in the elastic regime with adequate margin.
  • 💥 Estimated rupture torque (ultimate): ≈ 633.1 N·m — extrapolated from the Su/Sy ratio. Actual value depends on stress concentration, fatigue history, and temperature.
  • 📐 At 1° reference twist: τ_max = 5766.86 MPa, τ_yield = 121.17 MPa, ratio τ_yield/τ_max = 0.02. This ratio indicates how far the shaft geometry is from yielding at 1° of elastic twist.
  • 🎵 Torsional natural frequencies: f₁ = 24.98 Hz, f₂ = 8069.57 Hz, f₃ = 12813.50 Hz. Verify that driveline excitation frequencies (engine orders, joint angles) do not coincide with these values to avoid resonance.
  • ⚖️ Estimated shaft mass: 0.699 kg (solid cylinder model, ρ = 7850 kg/m³). Consider this value when evaluating unsprung mass, balance, and inertia targets.
